Chapter II

The Recovery

A crater in the sand. A hull with no seams. And behind a hydraulic door — something that wasn't human.

The Crash Site

The recovery team reached the impact zone by helicopter. What they found, according to the leaked dossier, was unlike anything in their training.

The object had struck the desert floor at a shallow 25-degree angle, embedding itself into a large crater. The sand surrounding the impact point was fused — melted into glass-like sheets by intense heat. One telescopic landing leg protruded from the side of the craft, apparently deployed by the impact itself.

The craft itself was described as a seamless, polished metallic hull — no visible rivets, no weld lines, no panel joints. Its surface appeared to be a single continuous piece of material. The first helicopter to reach the site at approximately 500 feet altitude experienced immediate engine trouble, its instruments destabilized by what the report called "an intense electro-magnetic field emanating from the object."

Five crew members in that first helicopter were killed when it stalled and crashed. Subsequent vehicles approaching by ground also reported electronic failures.

A paint-like compound was eventually received at the site and painted on the object, which appeared to neutralise the magnetic field.

The object was eventually conveyed to an Air Force Base and was taken to the sixth level underground. At this time it was totally intact.

The Entities

Behind the Door

According to the leaked account, while the recovery team and scientists examined the exterior of the craft, they heard a noise from inside. A small opening — a doorway — had appeared in the hull's side. Attempts to open it with hydraulic gear failed. Eventually, the door opened on its own.

Two small beings emerged — staggering, disoriented. They were immediately seized by security personnel. A makeshift medical holding area was established. One of the entities appeared seriously injured. When a military doctor approached, the other entity attacked — leaving deep scratches on the doctor's face and chest with what the report described as "claws."

Where Else Has This Been Seen?

The entity description in the Kalahari file is not a bizarre one-off. It sits within a recurring pattern of reported encounters spanning decades and continents:

🎓

Ariel School, 1994

62 children in Zimbabwe describe small beings with huge dark eyes and tight black suits. Five years after the alleged Kalahari crash, 800 km northeast.

Out of Africa

The leaked timeline claims that on 23 June 1989 — six weeks after the crash — the craft and the entities were flown out of South Africa in two Galaxy C-2 transport aircraft, accompanied by American Air Force personnel. Destination: Wright-Patterson AFB, Dayton, Ohio.

Wright-Patterson has been the nexus of crash-retrieval lore since Roswell in 1947. The Kalahari account places itself squarely in that tradition — whether by design or by coincidence.
