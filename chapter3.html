Chapter III

The Documents

Five pages bearing the SAAF crest. A memorandum codenamed BLUE FIRE. And 14 spelling mistakes that changed everything.

The Leak

During the first week of July 1989 — barely two months after the alleged crash — a man calling himself "Mr. X" began distributing a document to British UFO researchers. It consisted of five pages, each stamped with the South African Air Force crest, each marked Top Secret.

The document described the full intercept, crash, recovery, entity encounter, and transfer to Wright-Patterson AFB. It named Operation Silver Diamond as the joint SAAF-USAF recovery mission. It referenced Thor-2. It described the beings in clinical detail.

Quest International, a UK-based UFO research magazine, devoted an entire special edition to the case in May/June 1993. The document was republished, analysed, and debated across the UFO research community for years.

The Memorandum

BLUE FIRE

Perhaps the most intriguing document connected to the case is the "BLUE FIRE" memorandum — a three-page security advisory dated 28 July 1991, purportedly originating from NRO/Central Security Service.

BLUE FIRE

MEMORANDUM FOR RECORD

FROM: NRO/CENTRAL SECURITY SERVICE

SUBJECT: SPECIAL SECURITY ADVISORY/BLUE FIRE

ATTENTION: Commanders Net — ROYAL Ops, COSMIC Ops, MAJ Ops, MAJI Ops, COMINT Ops, COMSEC Ops, ELINT Ops, HUMINT Ops...

Distribution list includes: Groom Lake MOC, Dreamland MOC, Sally Corridor MOC, Red Flag MOC, Sea Spray SOG, Blackjack Team, Roulette Team, Aqua Tech SOG...

The memo ordered a full base shutdown — "BLUE FIRE" condition — at what appears to be Area 51 and associated installations. It names specific civilian UFO researchers who were to be monitored during a scheduled event in Rachel, Nevada, and threatens prosecution under the National Security Acts for any personnel who communicated about the advisory.

What makes BLUE FIRE significant to the investigation is not necessarily its authenticity — it remains unverified. It's the architecture of the document. In 2024, a whistleblower report titled "Immaculate Constellation" described a classified USAP (Unacknowledged Special Access Program) using strikingly similar distribution structures, security classification language, and compartmentalisation patterns.

The question this raises: was the BLUE FIRE memo modelled on real classified document formats that weren't publicly known until decades later? Or is the similarity coincidental?

The Phone Call

"General Brunel, Surely It's a Straight Forward Question"

Among the most dramatic elements in the leaked dossier is a transcribed telephone conversation between a private investigator and the Squadron Leader who allegedly fired on the object. When connected to a "General Brunel" at Wright-Patterson, the conversation turns tense:

QUESTION: This document does not say how many times you fired at the object.

REPLY: Who did you say you were sir.

QUESTION: General Brunel, surely Squadron Leader it's a straight forward question, how many times did you fire at the darn thing.

REPLY: I fired once sir, could you hang on so that I can go to a safe phone.

QUESTION: That won't be necessary Squadron Leader, you have told me what I wanted to know. Goodbye.

This exchange — if genuine — would be extraordinary. But its very narrative polish raises questions. Is this a real transcript, or a piece of crafted drama designed to lend verisimilitude to a fabricated dossier?

The Informants

Dr. Henry and the Second Source

The dossier claims a second intelligence officer in South Africa — unnamed — had independently confirmed the crash and recovery, claiming access to photographs and a 50-page telex from Wright-Patterson AFB. He stated he could obtain the papers but stressed it was extremely dangerous.

The informant's contact, identified as "Dr. Henry," reportedly took temporary residence near Wright-Patterson and made a sworn statement confirming the story. He claimed hieroglyphic markings were found inside the craft and that military specialists had partially deciphered them.

None of these secondary claims have been independently verified. They exist solely within the framework of the leaked dossier.
