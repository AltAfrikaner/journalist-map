Prologue

On a clear autumn evening in 1989, something appeared on South African military radar — moving at impossible speed on a northwest trajectory toward the Kalahari Desert. Two Mirage interceptors were scrambled. What happened next has been debated, denied, and buried for over three decades.

Was it a genuine encounter with the unknown? A classified weapons test gone wrong? Or one of the Cold War's most elaborate disinformation campaigns — designed to hide South Africa's nuclear secrets behind the perfect cover story: a crashed UFO?

This investigation doesn't choose sides. It lays out what we know, what the documents claim, and what the verified history of the era makes possible. You decide what's real.

A Note on Method

This project weaves verified Cold War history with unresolved anomalous claims, using a tiered evidence system. Every assertion is tagged by source quality:

Tier 1 — Documentary / Primary Tier 2 — Corroborated Tier 3 — Testimony / Unverified

We don't tell you what to believe. We show you what's on file — and what's missing from it.
