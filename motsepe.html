Active and Unresolved Matters

No South African prosecutor, court, or commission has made a corruption finding against Motsepe. The following matters are documented in court filings and public records.

● ACTIVE | $195 MILLION

🇹🇿 Tanzania Graphite Dispute

Pula Group (led by Mary Stith, daughter of former US Ambassador Charles Stith) v. ARM, ARC, Motsepe, ARCH. Filed 2023. Alleges breach of 2019 confidentiality agreement. Default judgment granted after Motsepe, ARM, ARCH failed to appear at December 2023 hearing. ARC filed ex parte application in Johannesburg (2025). Pula calls this "forum shopping." Graphite is a critical mineral for battery technology.

● CLASSIFIED | IPID REPORT

🇿🇦 Phala Phala Cattle Allegation

March 2022: Motsepe pays R4.7 million for four Ankole cows at Ramaphosa's farm auction (one cow: R2.1 million). June 2022: private citizen files criminal complaint alleging the purchase was a bribery mechanism. Same month: Arthur Fraser (former State Security Agency chief) files complaint naming bribery circuit across mining and energy sectors. Independent Police Investigative Directorate (IPID) report was classified Top Secret. Partially declassified February 2026. Contents remain inaccessible.

● HISTORIC | 2004-2018

⚒️ Modikwa Community Conflict

Modikwa platinum mine (50:50 joint venture between ARM and Anglo American Platinum) in Limpopo. Communities alleged water poisoning, noise pollution, non-distribution of benefits. Human rights lawyer: Motsepe "uses the fact that he is black to turn the screws on the community." April 2018: bus transporting night-shift workers petrol-bombed, killing six. Community leaders: the mine had been "looking the other way."
