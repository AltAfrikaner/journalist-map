What This Page Maps

The Open Society Foundation in South Africa.
For the first time, assembled in full.

The Open Society Foundation was founded in South Africa in April 1993. Its first chairman was Frederik van Zyl Slabbert — who had co-organised the 1987 Dakar Conference at which George Soros personally financed the meeting of South Africans with the banned ANC. That origin event is the foundation's documented starting point in this country.

Over thirty years, OSF-SA invested more than $230 million across more than 750 South African grantees. The investments span media organisations, universities, civil society NGOs, legal advocacy bodies, and individual personnel who moved between OSF and state institutions. No single news report has ever assembled these connections in one place. This page does that.

This is not a claim of conspiracy. It is a documentation of documented relationships: grants, personnel, shared directorships, commissioned research, and co-funding arrangements. The map exists because the documents exist. Every node has a source. Every edge has a reference.

What is OSF?

The Open Society Foundation (formerly Open Society Foundations) was founded by George Soros in 1979 in Hungary and expanded globally through the 1980s and 1990s. In South Africa, OSF-SA was registered in Cape Town in April 1993 — one year before the democratic transition. It operates as a US 501(c)(3) with a bifurcated structure: US-restricted activities run through the New York entity; activities restricted under US tax law are routed through OSF Switzerland (Zug entity). Total global spend exceeds $25 billion.

The Structural Question

The most important finding is not any single grant. It is the pattern: the same foundation that funds Africa Check (the fact-checker) also funds the civil society organisations those fact-checkers cover — and the academic pipeline (Wasserman, UCT, Wits) that defines what counts as disinformation. When the funder, the fact-checker, the subject, and the definer of truth are all within the same network, the question of independence cannot be answered by any individual disclosure.

What the Network Reveals.

01
Africa Check Is the Chokepoint
Africa Check is Wits-housed, OSF-funded (12%), Gates-funded (13%), and Luminate-funded (8%). Its IFCN certification determines which content Meta suppresses globally. The person who co-founded Africa Check (Peter Cunliffe-Jones) now sits on the IFCN board. The pipeline from OSF grant to Facebook content removal is three nodes long.
02
The Wasserman Academic Pipeline
Prof Herman Wasserman holds the Stellenbosch Chair and edits the two journals that define 'disinformation' for African media. He is commissioned by Africa Check, funded by IDRC (R4.5M), and his UCT research classified farm attack Facebook pages as 'problematic information.' His academic framework is the intellectual scaffold that justifies OSF-funded content suppression.
03
The Personnel Revolving Door
Patrick Gaspard (OSF President 2019-2023) was US Ambassador to South Africa 2013-2016 — the same year OSF-SA invested $931,650 in Wits ACCAI. Simon Allison moved from Daily Maverick → Mail & Guardian → ISS Africa (OSF-funded) → The Continent (NED-funded). One person carrying the same editorial framework across four institutions in the OSF/NED-adjacent network.
04
The $29M Constitutionalism Fund
OSF, Ford Foundation, and Atlantic Philanthropies co-created a $29M fund in 2015 to sustain constitutionalism work after Atlantic's exit. The selection panel was chaired by former ConCourt Justice Yvonne Mokgoro. Lawyers for Human Rights is the primary grantee. Three LHR-connected individuals signed the 2025 'Not In Our Name' letter to Washington.
05
The Dakar 1987 Origin
George Soros personally financed the 1987 Dakar Conference at Van Zyl Slabbert's request — 'he agreed on the spot' (OSF 30-Year Report, March 2025, Alex Soros foreword). Slabbert became OSF-SA's founding chairman in 1993. Max du Preez (Dakar attendee) founded Vrye Weekblad there. The 2025 letter network and the 1987 conference share the same institutional origin.
06
The Swiss Bifurcation
OSF operates a bifurcated structure: the US 501(c)(3) entity for activities compatible with US tax law, and OSF Switzerland (Zug) for activities restricted under US tax-exempt law. Internal OSF documents from the DCLeaks 'Karl' intranet dump confirm this bifurcation was deliberate — not structural necessity. The Zug entity enables disbursements the New York entity cannot make.
