Video Generation

Best workflow: generate a perfect still image first, then use image-to-video to add motion. Specify camera movement precisely.

Currently the leader in physics-aware generation. Ideal for: aerial desert flyovers, military vehicle movement, atmospheric effects like dust and heat haze. Free daily credits (~66). Character consistency across shots is excellent.

Advanced camera controls (pan, tilt, zoom, orbit). Multi-Motion Brush for animating specific regions. Best for the radar room sequences, document close-ups, and any shot requiring precise camera choreography. Standard plan: $15/month.

Recommended Workflow

How to Produce This Documentary

Step 1
Script & Storyboard
Use the chapter narratives as your script. Each image placeholder in the story is a shot in your storyboard. List every scene with its AI image prompt.
Step 2
Generate Still Frames
Run each prompt through Leonardo.ai or Midjourney. Generate 4-8 variations per scene. Select the best. This is your cheapest creative phase — perfect the stills before adding motion.
Step 3
Animate with Image-to-Video
Feed your selected stills into Kling 3.0, Runway, or Veo. Add camera movement: slow zoom on documents, aerial push across desert, dolly around crash site. 5-10 second clips.
Step 4
Record or Generate Narration
Use ElevenLabs with a deep, measured documentary voice. Alternatively, record your own narration and use ElevenLabs for voice enhancement/translation.
Step 5
Sound Design
Layer Suno-generated ambient score underneath. Add Freesound effects — radar beeps, radio static, jet engines, desert wind. Build tension through sound.
Step 6
Edit & Assemble
Bring everything into CapCut or Descript. Cut to narration rhythm. Add classified-document overlays, text transitions, and colour grading (desaturated, amber-tinted, film grain).
