35 Years — Three Simultaneous Pipelines

Political leader cultivation, family and business network embedding, and institutional infrastructure installation. Three parallel tracks, one convergence point: 2027. Click any event to read the full sourced detail.

🇨🇭🇨🇭 1990
🔴 De Klerk Unbans the ANC at Davos
On 2 February 1990, President F.W. de Klerk announced the unbanning of the ANC, the Communist Party, the PAC, and release of political prisoners. The announcement was first heard by more than 1,000 of the world's top business and political leaders gathered in the WEF concourse in Davos, Switzerland. It was not heard first in Cape Town. Not in Johannesburg. In Davos.
Klaus Schwab had been engineering this moment for years. He sent a 26-year-old WEF executive named Frederic Sicre to South Africa in 1990, carrying 40 kilograms of WEF literature, with the explicit instruction to identify future South African leaders and bring them into the WEF network. The choice of Davos as the announcement venue was not accidental — it signalled to global capital that South Africa's transition was underway and investment-safe. The WEF's own historical archives describe Schwab's role in the transition as central, noting his travel to South Africa "several times" to arrange the 1992 joint appearance of Mandela and De Klerk.
🎪 Political Pipeline
🇨🇭🇨🇭 1992
🤯 Mandela Reverses Nationalisation at Davos
Nelson Mandela attended the WEF Annual Meeting and abandoned the ANC's 30-year policy of nationalising banks, mines, and strategic industries. He had walked out of prison in 1990 insisting the policy was "inconceivable" to abandon. At Davos, after meetings arranged by Schwab — including a private meeting with 🇨🇳 China's Communist Party Premier Li Peng — he changed his mind.
Tito Mboweni (later Finance Minister) documented Mandela's account of the meeting with Communist Party leaders of China and Vietnam at Davos: "They told him frankly: 'We are currently striving to privatise state enterprises and invite private enterprise into our economies. We are Communist Party governments, and you are a leader of a national liberation movement. Why are you talking about nationalisation?' It was those decisive moments which made him think about the need for our movement to seriously rethink the issue." The WEF's own records note: "Coming from China, that message made more of an impression than any other." Mandela himself described the hostility to nationalisation from the assembled industrialists as something that "could be cut with a knife." The ANC's 1992 policy conference, held after Davos, produced a document called "Ready to Govern" from which the word "nationalisation" had been entirely removed. The foundational economic settlement of post-apartheid South Africa was shaped at a private Swiss gathering — not at a constitutional negotiation, not at an ANC conference, not in Parliament.

🇨🇭 1993
👑 Ramaphosa Selected as WEF Inaugural Class Member
Cyril Ramaphosa, serving as the ANC's chief negotiator at South Africa's democratic transition talks, was selected as the inaugural member of the WEF's Global Leaders for Tomorrow program — not yet a minister, not yet a deputy president, 25 years before he became president.
The Global Leaders for Tomorrow program (renamed Young Global Leaders in 2003) is the WEF's primary mechanism for cultivating pre-power leaders. Alumni include French President Emmanuel Macron, former New Zealand PM Jacinda Ardern, and former Canadian PM Justin Trudeau. The program selects individuals under 40 who show leadership potential, brings them into regular WEF contact with the world's most powerful executives and policymakers, and immerses them in WEF policy frameworks before they gain executive authority. Ramaphosa's selection in 1993 — when he was a trade union leader turned constitutional negotiator — placed him inside this network 26 years before his presidency began. The cultivation was longer than most people's entire careers. Patrice Motsepe was also recognised in the same program in subsequent years.

Confirmed in WEF published archives, confirmed by multiple witnesses. The most explicit statement of WEF influence on South Africa's transition ever made by a South African head of state.

The 1999 farewell statement is not a footnote or a courtesy. It is an explicit causal attribution: the World Economic Forum shaped South Africa's post-apartheid economic architecture. ▶ How It Works

The Policy Pre-Framing Machine

A consistent three-stage mechanism has converted WEF policy frameworks into South African government commitments across 35 years. No stage passes through parliamentary debate or public vote. The mechanism is self-reinforcing: installed infrastructure creates demand for new WEF frameworks to fill it.

1

🇨🇭 WEF Articulates the Framework

The World Economic Forum, funded by 1,000 multinational corporations paying up to CHF 600,000 per year, publishes policy vocabulary and frameworks. These are developed across its Global Agenda Councils, regional summits, and annual Davos programs. Examples: "Fourth Industrial Revolution" (Schwab, 2016 book), "Stakeholder Capitalism" (Schwab, 2021 book), "Just Transition" (WEF/COP26 architecture), "Intelligent Age" (WEF Annual Meeting theme 2025). The WEF does not govern. It shapes vocabulary and defines the parameters of legitimate policy debate.

KEY PLAYERS: 🇨🇭 Klaus Schwab • WEF Global Agenda Councils • 1,000 corporate member companies
2

🇨🇭 Leaders Absorb the Vocabulary Before Taking Power

Pre-power leaders cultivated through WEF programs absorb the policy vocabulary. Average lag between WEF articulation and ANC government adoption: 12–36 months. The evidence is textual: Ramaphosa's 2025 Davos address adopts WEF vocabulary verbatim — "Solidarity, Equality, Sustainability" is the G20 presidency theme, indistinguishable from WEF communications. The government's official 2026 Davos participation document uses: "responsible deployment of frontier technologies," "inclusive cross-industry digitisation and decarbonisation," "just and sustainable energy transition aligned with global climate objectives" — all WEF vocabulary adopted as ANC domestic policy language.

KEY PLAYERS: 🇨🇭 Ramaphosa (YGL 1993) • WEF bilateral meetings annually • SA delegation at Davos every year
3

🇨🇭 Institutional Agreements Lock the Framework Into the State

Formal governmental agreements install WEF frameworks inside South African state infrastructure. Compliance obligations are created. External financial dependencies are established. Once signed, these agreements cannot be reversed without reputational and financial consequences. Examples: C4IR at CSIR (2021), JETP 2.8B conditional finance (2021–2025), C4IR at University of Pretoria (2025), WEF Spring Davos hosting (2027). The university will "leverage the Forum's global communities and initiatives" — by contractual commitment.

KEY PLAYERS: 🇨🇭 Dr Blade Nzimande (SACP) • 🇨🇭 University of Pretoria • 🇨🇭 CSIR • 🇪🇺🇬🇧🇫🇷🇩🇪 International Partners Group
